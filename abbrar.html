Their Journey

The Story So Far

School days are short. But some moments stretch into forever.

01

Chapter One

The First Notice

Science class. Shared corridor. Abrar was doing what he always did — walking with that slightly-too-confident stride when he first noticed Jhoti laughing with her friends near the A-section door. It was just a laugh. But it was a loud, genuine, unfiltered laugh — and somehow it cut right through the noise of the hallway.

👀
"Wait... who is that?"
📝
"Can I borrow a pen?" (He had three.)
02

Chapter Two

The Excuse

Their schools shared a library period once a week. Abrar, armed with three perfectly good pens in his bag, still managed to find himself asking Jhoti to borrow one. Classic. She handed it over without a second glance — she was always like that, generous without needing a reason.

03

Chapter Three

The Friend Circle Chaos

Once word got around that Abrar and Jhoti had "talked," the school's internal gossip network activated at full speed. Both their friend groups became insufferable — teasing, winking across hallways, and staging increasingly dramatic "accidental" run-ins. It was embarrassing. It was also kind of sweet.

🤭
"Guys stop it — he's RIGHT THERE."
☁️
Rooftop during break. Quiet moments.
04

Chapter Four

The Quiet Afternoons

Eventually, the teasing faded into the background and what remained were the small, ordinary things — helping each other prep for Chemistry viva, trading notes after a particularly chaotic Physics lecture, and somehow always ending up near each other during school events. Not planned. Just comfortable.

The Friend Squad

The Fun Moments

When friends get involved, the teasing is real — and completely, embarrassingly delightful.

😭

The Hallway Walk

Every time Abrar walked past Section A, his friends would dramatically slow down and start whispering loudly. Jhoti's friends would giggle in response. Both groups pretended not to notice each other. It was painfully obvious.

"Bro walk NORMAL." — Abrar's best friend, every single time.
📱

The Screenshot Evidence

The group chat was a crime scene. Every seen-but-not-replied, every quick reply, every two-minute response time — analysed, screenshotted, and submitted as court evidence by friends who had absolutely nothing better to do.

"He replied in 45 seconds. Case CLOSED." — the friend group, as detectives.
🎭

The Name Slip

Once Abrar accidentally called out "Jhoti" when his teacher asked a question — meaning to say something completely different. The class went silent. Then erupted. He somehow survived the rest of the day without making eye contact with anyone.

"We do NOT talk about this." — Abrar, for the next two weeks.
📖

The Note Exchange

They started sharing notes — totally normal, totally academic. Jhoti's friends took one look at the neatly written notes with star stickers on the cover and immediately declared it a love letter situation. It was a Chemistry handout. But also, kind of.

"He drew a tiny rocket on the margin. ROCKET." — Jhoti's bestie, as evidence.
🎤

The School Talent Show

Both participated in the annual school event — coincidentally performing back to back. Their friend groups, sitting on opposite ends of the auditorium, somehow coordinated cheering at the same time. Nobody planned it. Everyone denied it later.

"I was cheering for myself." — every single friend from both groups.
🌧️

The Rainy Day Cover

It rained unexpectedly after school. Abrar had an umbrella. Jhoti's group didn't. You can already see where this is going. What followed was three minutes of perfectly normal umbrella sharing and six months of relentless teasing from everyone who witnessed it.

"I still have the photo." — the one friend who always has the photo.
"School days feel endless when you're in them — until they end. Then you'd give anything to walk those corridors one more time."

— From the hallways of Ispahani
